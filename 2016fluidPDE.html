2016 Fall: Topics on PDEs in Fluid Dynamics



Short Bio:
  • Instructor: Professor Lifeng Zhao
  • Time: 4(8,9,10), 6(2,3,4), Week 1-18.
  • Classroom:@5305, East Campus.
  • Grade: 50%Homework+50%Presence
    • Prerequisites:
  • Real Analysis: Convergence Theorems and L^p Theory;
  • Functional Analysis;
  • PDE: Fourier Characterization of Sobolev Spaces, Differential Equation 2;
  • Harmonic Analysis: Interpolation, Calderon-Zygmund Singular Integrals, Littlewood-Paley Theory.
    • Reference Books and Materials:
  • [1] Majda, Bertozzi: Vorticity and Incompressible Flows;
  • [2] James C. Robinson, Jose L. Rodrigo, Witold Sadowski: Three-dimensional Navier-Stokes Equation: Classical Theory;
  • [3] Bahouri等: Fourier Analysis and Nonlinear Partial Differential Equations, Chapter 1-2;
  • [4] 苗长兴、吴家宏、章志飞: Littlewood-Paley理论及其在流体动力学方程中的应用, 前两章;
  • [5] Christian Zillinger: Linear Inviscid Damping for Monotone Shear Flows, arxiv 1410.7347, preprint;
  • [6] Jacob Bedrossian, Nader Masmoudi: Inviscid damping and the asymptotic stability of planar shear flows in the 2D Euler equations, IHES, 2015.
    • Outline
    • Chapter 1: Prerequisites of Harmonic Analysis:
        Calderon-Zygmund Singular Integrals;
        Littlewood-Paley Theory and Heat Flow Characterization;
        Littlewood-Paley Characterization of Sobolev spaces and Besov Spaces;
        Bony's Paraproduct Decomposition;
    • Chapter 2: Basic Theory about Euler's Equation
        Different Forms of Euler's Equation: Leray's Projection, Vorticity, Particle Orbits;
        Local Wellposedness of Euler's Equation;
        Benjamin-Kato-Majda Blow-up Criteria;
        Relations between Weak solution and Strong solution;
    • Chapter 3: Basic Theory about Navier-Stokes Equation
        Fujita-Kato Semilinear Method;
        Mild Solution, Leray-Hopf Solution, Strong-Weak Uniqueness;
    • Chapter 4: Inviscid Damping and Asymptotic Stability
        Spectral Stability
        Linear Inviscid Damping for Monotone Shear Flows of 2D Euler
    • Chapter 5: Inviscid damping and the asymptotic stability of planar shear flows in the 2D Euler equations in T×R
        Prerequisites: Gevery Spaces, Paraproduct and Estimates in Calculus
        Reduction of 2D Euler in New Coordinates;
        Elliptic Estimates: Toy Model Method;
        Estimates of Transport Items;
        Estimates of Reaction Items;
        Estimates of Remainders;
        Bootstrap and Conclusion;
