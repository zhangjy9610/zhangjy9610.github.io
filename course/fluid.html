MA05179 Nonlinear PDEs in Fluid Dynamics

Basic Information:

Instructor: Lifeng Zhao Email: zhaolf@ustc.edu.cn

Classroom: 5305, East Campus, USTC

Time: 16:00-18:20 on Thursday, 08:40-11:00 on Saturday

Prerequisites: Real Analysis, Functional Analysis, Differential Equations II, Harmonic Analysis

Main Contexts:

1. Basic Theory of Euler Equation and Navier-Stokes Equation: Conservation Law, Geometric Theory of Euler's Equation, Strong Solution, Leray-Hopf Weal Solution and Mild Solution.

2. Non-uniqueness of the solution to Euler's equation: H-Principle, Baire's Category Method.

3. Inviscid Damping: Besov Spaces and Paraproduct Decomposition, Theory of Nonlinear Stability.

4. Semigroup Theory and the Blow-up of Parabolic PDEs.

Reference Books:

[1] A. J. Majda, A. L. Bertozzi: Vorticity and Incompressible Flow, Cambridge Texts in Applied Mathematics, Cambridge University Press, 2002.

[2] 苗长兴、吴家宏、章志飞: Littlewood-Paley理论及其在流体动力学方程中的应用，科学出版社，2012.

[3] H. Bahouri, J.-Y. Chemin, R. Danchin: Fourier Analysis and Nonlinear Partial Differential Equtions, Springer Press, 2011.

[4] J. Bedrossian, N. Masmoudi: Inviscid Damping and the Asymptotic Stability of Planar Shear Flows in the 2D Euler Equations, 2015.
