2017 Spring: Linear and Semilinear Dispersive PDEs



Short Bio:
  • Instructor: Professor Lifeng Zhao
  • Time: 2(8,9), 4(8,9), Week 1-16.
  • Classroom:@5207, East Campus.
  • Grade: 40%Homework+40%Presence+20%Final Exam
    • Prerequisites:
  • Real Analysis: Convergence Theorems and L^p Theory;
  • Functional Analysis;
  • PDE: Fourier Characterization of Sobolev Spaces, Differential Equation 2;
  • Harmonic Analysis: Interpolation, Whitney Decomposition, Littlewood-Paley Theory;
  • Differential Manifolds: Lie Derivatives, Symplectic Manifolds;
  • Riemann Geometry: Covariant Derivatives, Bianchi Identity;
    • Reference Books and Materials:
  • [1] Terence Tao: Nonlinear Dispersive Equations: Local and Global Analysis, AMS,2008;
  • [2] Christopher D. Sogge: Letcures on Nonlinear Wave Equations;
  • [3] Herbert Koch, Daniel Tataru, Monica Visan: Dispersive Equations Nonlinear Waves;
  • [4] 苗长兴、张波: 偏微分方程的调和分析方法;
  • [5] Elias M. Stein: Harmonic Analysis, Chapter 8;
  • [6] Jonathan Luk: Introduction to Nonlinear Wave Equations.
  • [7] Sergiu Klainerman: Lecture Notes in Analysis, 2011, Princeton.
  • [8] Carlos E. Kenig，G. Ponce，L. Vega: Oscillatory integrals and regularity of dispersive equations, Indiana University Mathematics Journal, 1991, 40(1):33-69.
  • [9]Pierre Germain, Fabio Pusateri, Frédéric Rousset: Asymptotic stability of solitons for mKdV, Advances in Mathematics, 2016.
    • Outline
    • Chapter 1: Prerequisites:
        Distribution Theory and Fourier Transform;
        1st Type of Oscillatory Integrals;
    • Chapter 2: Derivation of Dispersive PDEs
        Hamiltonian Formulation;
        Lagrangian Formulation;
        Water wave and Maxwell-Euler Equation;
    • Chapter 3: Basic Theory about Linear Dispersive Equations
        Fundamental Solution to Wave Equation;
        Finite Propagation Speed, Huygen's Principle;
        Commuting Vector Fields Method: Klainerman-Sobolev Inequalities;
        Abstract Strichartz Estimates;
        Strichartz Estimates for Wave Equation;
        Strichatz Estimates for Schrodinger Equation;
        Christ-Kiselev Lemma, Keel-Tao's proof of endpoint Strichartz Estimates.
    • Chapter 4: Semilinear Schrodinger Equation
        Local Wellposedness of NLS with dimension higher than 3;
        Blow-up Criteria and Critical Spaces of Global Wellposedness;
    • Chapter 5: Soliton Theory and Dispersive Equations
